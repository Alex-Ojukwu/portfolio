# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, potential clients, and university assessors evaluating Alex-Ojukwu Nduka's software engineering skills. They arrive from a CV link, LinkedIn, or GitHub, skim in under a minute, and decide whether to reach out via the contact form.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Alex-Ojukwu Nduka — full-stack developer and AI/ML engineer. Showcases projects (Xylo AI, CafeRoute, Doctor's Booking, etc.), skills, education, and certifications, and converts visitors into contact-form submissions. Deployed on Vercel.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, immersive, confident. The site should feel like the title screen of a premium streaming production — the visitor is watching Alex's work, not reading a résumé. Dark, monochrome, motion-led.

## Anti-references

- Generic light-mode template portfolios (the create-next-app / Tailwind-starter look this replaced).
- Gradient-text-everywhere indigo/violet SaaS styling.
- Busy multi-color dashboards; anything that competes with the background video.

## Design Principles

1. **One screen, one idea** — the hero is a single full-viewport cinematic statement; sections below get the same single-focus pacing.
2. **Monochrome commitment** — pure black surface, white ink, liquid-glass controls; the only solid-color element per screen is the primary CTA (white on black). Reference: Vercel pure-black monochrome + Apple TV title-screen glass.
3. **Motion is orchestrated, not scattered** — one staggered blur-fade-up page-load choreography; micro-interactions stay subtle. Reduced motion is always respected.
4. **The work is the imagery** — background video and real project screenshots carry the visuals; no decorative illustration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast (≥4.5:1 body text) on dark surfaces — verify against video backgrounds, not just black.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` disables entrance animation and pauses the background video.
- Keyboard-navigable nav and form; visible focus states on glass controls.
